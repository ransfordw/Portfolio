A little about me


Hello World! I am relatively new to the world of web development and coding. I grew up on the southside of Indianapolis raising chickens and sheep for 4-H and exploring the wilds that were my back yard. I was incredibly fascinated with nature and how things grew; I was always walking around with my head down looking at whatever I could find. We had an aquarium that we kept crawdads in from te creek that we would watch and learn how they hunted, ate, and lived. This close proximity to livestock and our woods helped foster my curiosity for biology. In high school, I found myself taking elective sciences whenever I could. It seemed I couldn't gain enough knowledge on the subject to satisfy my curiosity. Dissections, designing my own experiments, and field trips surveying local streams all gave my hands on experiences that spurred on my curiosity for biology.

I also found my first job in high school. I had done several odd jobs but eventually got hired at a local Chick-fil-A. There I learned the value of team work, learning how to function with people that never did their work, drove you crazy, or that you enjoyed working with so much that you had to work hard to stay focused. It was a great place to fail and learn. Our owner set a number of us down shortly after the 2008 recession and spoke with us about his goals to not let anyone go because of the financial state of the economy. He then pointed out areas he had seen in us that warrented improvements, hinting that if he had to let people go that those things would be considered. We all shaped up our acts after that but I learned how important it is to care for your team and share you goals with them. A skill that helped me excel as a manager at that same Chick-fil-A a few years later.

In college my interest in biology and sciences continued to grow. After a year of being an indescisive freshman I declared a major in biology and started trying to catch up to my class. I bit off more than I could chew and my grades reflected that. Moving forward I took more care to grab a manageable workload and started learning better boundaries between studying and resting. I ended up being one of the few students I knew who enjoyed organic chemistry and loved taking advanced biology courses. I loved learning the details of how life worked, what chemicals made us up, how our DNA codes our proteins to carry out tasks both simple and complex, and how the slightest deviation could throw everything off. By the time I was a senior I had developed a deep love for learning new things.

Shortly after college I was married and back at Chick-fil-A. Much to my dismay, my biology degree could not guarantee the income level I had been promised going into college without further schooling and engagement rings and loans are costly. Chick-fil-A could at least give me steady income, but also so much more. In my three years I spent there after college that same owner continued to give me opportunities to grow and learn. I moved up through the ranks until I was responsibile for managing the daytime operations and overseeing food safety procedures for two locations. In managing people, I learned to invest time in people as individuals and that in turn makes a more cohesive and productive team, that unresolved conflict can undoe great amounts of work, and that setting measurable goals are more effective that broad visions.

Eventually I knew my time with Chick-fil-A would come to a close. Ten hour days, 4:00am alarms, and working nearly every saturday took a toll on me and my time with my family. I searched for six months for jobs that my degree would qualify me for but had no luck. After several conversations, a good friend of mine convinced me to try my hand at coding, and look at this! I've made a website! I had no idea how much I would enjoy the challenge and problemsolving that comes with this field and I am eager to find a job where I can keep learning and developing myself.
