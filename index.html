Lots of cool stuf about how Ransford is the best possible option for your company. He likes birds. He can bake delicious scones. He likes camping and fishing. One time, he went on a hike at night with some friends. As they were walking through the woods he would turn around periodically and count to make sure he had everyone with him. There were several new "woodsmen" with him and he was fearful some might get lost.

After they had been hiking for about 45 minutes he decided he needed to do another quick count. When he turned around his head lamp caught to eyes in the distance, glowing back at him. Being familiar with the area, he assumed it was a raccoon or coyote or something and kept walking. Ten minutes or so go by and he turns around again just to check. The eyes were still there, closer than the last time. He thought that was odd and atypical of a lone coyote or raccoon and started to get nervous. There weren't any larger animals in southern Indiana so he figured it was someones dog or something that was lost. Still, a lost dog could become an issue, espcially if it was hungry or scared. He kept walking but kept an ear out just in case.

He kept checking his rear as he walked, trying to keep everyone focused and on pace to get back to camp a little quicker than he originally anticipated. And each time he looked back, the eyes were closer and bigger until he could see them come up alongside the group. A wide swooping gait accompanied them that had to belong to a creature much larger than he had originally expected. The only animal he knew of that would walk like that, alone in the woods at night was a mountain lion, but in southern Indiana that seemed a far cry from the truth. Still, he quickened his pace not wanting to find out.

Just a few minutes pass when one of the other hikers comes up beside him and quitely says, "Ransford, you know were being followed right?" He nodded yes, asked her to stay calm so the rest of the group wouldn't react in a way that would provoke anything. He knew they'd be back to the road soon and hoped that the road and lights would keep whatever it was from following them further.

They made it back and as soon as he felt they were safe, he told the group what had been going on. Shocked and largely in disbelief, they were relieved to be done with the hike but thought it was likely a coyote or something and that his nerves got the better of him. Two days later, the local papers told a story of the DNR trapping and collaring a mountain lion in the same forrest that they had been hiking in the next morning.
