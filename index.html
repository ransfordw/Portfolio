A little about me


Quick learning, teamwork, and excellence – these are the three qualities I readily offer to any environment. Though I have only recently been immersed in the world of computer coding, I have discovered that my past experiences have beautifully prepared me for a world driven by constant change, creative thinking, and problem solving.

I grew up on the south side of Indianapolis raising chickens and sheep for 4-H and exploring the wilds that were in my backyard. I was incredibly fascinated with nature and how things grew; it seemed I couldn't gain enough knowledge to satisfy my curiosity. I surrounded myself with scientific study throughout high school and college, and by the time I ended my academic career at Indiana University I had developed a deep love for learning. My mind is always ready to learn something new, whether it is a new coding skillset in my workplace or a new hobby – bonsai cultivation, Japanese language, or bird watching – in my free time.

Working through high school at my local Chick-fil-A taught me the value of teamwork: learning how to function with people that were both similar to me and vastly different. It was a perfect place to fail and grow. I learned how important it is to care for your team and to share your goals with them—a skill that helped me excel as a manager at that same business a few years later. After graduating college and marrying my wife I invested in Chick-Fil-A for 3 more years. In my time there post-grad the owner continued to give me opportunities to move up through the ranks until I was responsible for managing the daytime operations and overseeing food safety procedures for two locations. In managing people I learned to invest time in my colleagues as individuals, which in turn made a more cohesive and productive team. I experienced how unresolved conflict can undo great amounts of work, and I discovered that setting measurable goals are more effective than broad visions.

Eventually I knew my time with Chick-fil-A would come to a close. A good friend of mine convinced me to try my hand at coding, and I had no idea how much I would enjoy the challenge of problem solving that comes with this field! There are constant opportunities to create, revise, and improve until an excellent product is achieved. I am eager to find a working environment where I can continue learning and developing myself as a coder and as a professional.
